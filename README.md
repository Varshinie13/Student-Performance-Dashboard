# Student-Performance-Dashboard
Excel Data Analytics Project Comparing Student performance through data Cleaning, KPI Calculations, Pivot tables, and interactive Dashboard Visualization.

Student Performance Analysis: A Multi-Variable Socio-Economic Study:

Project Overview

This project leverages Microsoft Excel to analyze how various academic, personal, and socio-economic factors—such as study habits, sleep patterns, parental involvement, and internet access—influence student exam scores. As a data analyst, I focused on identifying correlations and providing actionable insights for educational stakeholders.

 Key Insights from the Dashboard:
 
 * The "Study-Sleep" Paradox: Students categorized as "High Study" don't always achieve the highest scores if sleep hours are critically low (under 5 hours). Optimal performance peaks when 7–8 hours of sleep are maintained, suggesting a diminishing return on study time without adequate recovery.
 
 * The Gender Performance Gap: The line chart reveals fluctuations in exam scores between genders across different study categories. Notably, in the "Improve Study" segment, there is a visible divergence that may suggest differing learning styles or environmental pressures.
 
 * Internet Access & Parental Synergy: While internet access generally correlates with higher scores, the data shows that Parental Involvement acts as a powerful moderator. High parental involvement can often "buffer" the negative impact of limited digital resources.
 
 📈 Statistical Analysis & Predictions:
 
 To elevate this from a descriptive dashboard to a predictive one, I applied the following statistical frameworks:
 
 1. Correlation vs. Causality Observation: There is a strong positive correlation between "Parental Involvement" and "Exam Score. Statistical Note: Using a Pearson Correlation Coefficient, we can quantify this relationship. However, as an analyst, I recognize that parental involvement may be a proxy for socio-economic status, necessitating further Multivariate Regression to isolate the true impact.

    
 2. Predictive Modeling (Linear Regression)By applying a Linear Regression model , we can predict a student's final score based on: Weekly study hours, Hours of sleep.
Prediction: For every additional hour of sleep (up to 8 hours), we observe a predicted increase of approximately $n$ points in the final exam score, holding study hours constant.

Project Outcome & Conclusion:

* The analysis successfully identified that academic success is a multifaceted outcome driven by the synergy of sleep hygiene, study consistency, and socio-economic support systems. 

* By visualizing these variables, the project provides a data-driven framework for educators to pinpoint "at-risk" student profiles based on low parental involvement and digital access gaps. 

* Statistically, the findings suggest that optimizing lifestyle factors, such as achieving 7–8 hours of sleep, can be as impactful as increasing study duration. Ultimately, this project demonstrates the power of Excel in transforming raw student data into actionable insights for personalized educational intervention.
